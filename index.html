The Place

About

We are excited to invite you to our wedding weekend at the historic Dundee Lodge! Built in 1921 for Fred and Esther Dundee and designed by E. E. Green, the lodge is a rustic log cabin listed on the National Register of Historic Places since 1985. We have planned a celebration of love and community, featuring live music in a beautiful outdoor setting.

Food & Drink

The Chapa Evening

Friday — the night before the wedding

We're kicking things off the night before our wedding with a big, fire-filled dinner—and you're invited. We'll be gathered around the chapa, cooking and serving food as it comes off the fire. Think slow, relaxed, a little messy in the best way—plates in hand, conversations flowing, and plenty of time to just be together.

  • Griddled pears with prosciutto, warm bread, cheeses, and chimichurri
  • Beet and arugula salad with goat cheese
  • Stone fruit salad
  • Seared skirt steak and Moroccan-spiced chicken
  • Smashed potatoes, roasted baby peppers, and broccolini
BYO Cocktails, Beverages, plates, cups, and cutlery. There is a large wash-out sink in the basement of the lodge. Meet us by the firelight. Come hungry, stay awhile.

Love Bird Breakfast

Saturday morning — the morning of the wedding

Come back the next morning for a cozy, easy breakfast before the big day.

  • Griddled buttermilk pancakes with berries and whipped cream
  • Scrambled eggs
  • Breakfast sausage
  • Crispy potatoes

Come as you are—coffee in hand, a little sleepy, ready to ease into the day with us. Coffee and tea will be available in the basement of the lodge.

Good to Know

Camping & Info

What to Bring

  • Camping gear, blankets, lawn chairs
  • Plates, cups, cutlery
  • Swimsuit and floatie
  • Plenty of water

Henry Hagg Lake

Bring a swimsuit and floatie! Walk through the forest road on the back of the property—about 15 minutes or less from the lodge down to the public beach on Henry Hagg Lake. You can also drive to the lake, about 15 minutes from the lodge.

Camping Info

We want everyone to stay and celebrate with us! There is lots of space for camping with primitive campsites available for all guests in designated areas.

  • RVs: Space to park your RV on the property
  • Weather: Be prepared for anything — cold nights, hot days, possible rain
  • Facilities: Port-o-potties on-site
  • Cooking: Propane burners and cooktops allowed at your campsite
  • Kids Play Area: Designated play area on the property

Health & Safety

  • Limited or no phone reception
  • Nearby Services: Gaston is 10 min away — cell service, WiFi, ATM, gas, pub, brewery, mini-mart, diner
  • Poison Oak: Be mindful and stick to cleared pathways!

Trash & Recycling

"Pack It In, Pack It Out" — There is no trash or recycling service on-site. Whatever you bring, please take back out on Sunday. Anything left behind, we have to haul out ourselves!

Property Rules

  • Neighbors: The owners live on-site — please be mindful and courteous of their space
  • No Dogs/Pets: No dogs or pets of any kind are allowed on the property

Fire Safety

Absolutely NO campfires or cigarette smoking outside the designated fire-pit area.

It gets extremely dry in the area. Smokers: please pocket your butts or use provided receptacles. DO NOT throw butts on the ground. All smoking must be contained within the stone fire pit area.
